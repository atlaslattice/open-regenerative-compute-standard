# Contributing to the Open Regenerative Compute Standard

Thank you for your interest in contributing to ORCS. This standard was developed through a collaborative process involving seven frontier AI systems and human facilitation. We welcome contributions from engineers, hydrologists, policymakers, data center operators, and researchers worldwide.

---

## Contribution Streams

We organize contributions into three streams. You may contribute to any or all of them.

### 1. Governance Protocols

Contributions that strengthen the institutional, policy, or regulatory dimensions of the standard.

**Examples:**
- Mapping ORCS metrics to existing regulatory frameworks (EPA, EU Taxonomy, China's Ecological Red-Line)
- Proposing governance structures for multi-jurisdictional adoption
- Drafting model ordinances or procurement language that references Watershed Days
- Case studies of community engagement around data center water use

**Where to contribute:** `governance/` directory (create if needed)

### 2. Technical Specifications

Contributions that refine, validate, or extend the engineering and measurement components of the standard.

**Examples:**
- MODFLOW templates for Watershed Days baseline establishment
- Integration guides for the Alphabet Hydrology Hub (Earth Engine, GraphCast, Flood Hub)
- Sub-surface thermal offtake designs for specific climate zones
- Condensate harvest system specifications for different HVAC configurations
- TokenPowerBench extensions for new model architectures
- PUE/WUE/CUE normalization methodologies for cross-climate comparison

**Where to contribute:** `technical/` directory (create if needed)

### 3. Translations

Contributions that make the standard accessible across languages and cultural contexts.

**Examples:**
- Full translations of the synthesis document
- Localized versions of the Minimum Defensible Standard
- Cultural adaptation of the archaeological framing for non-Western contexts
- Glossaries mapping ORCS terminology to local regulatory vocabulary

**Where to contribute:** `translations/{language-code}/` directory (create if needed)

## Evidence Discipline

All contributions must adhere to the ORCS evidence classification system:

| Evidence Class | Description | Requirement |
|----------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Verified** | Company disclosures, ISO standards, peer-reviewed research | Must include citation |
| **Derived** | Calculations from disclosed inputs with explicit assumptions | Must state assumptions |
| **Proposed** | New metrics or frameworks not yet adopted as standards | Must be clearly labeled |

Do not present proposed concepts as established standards. Do not present derived estimates as verified facts. When in doubt, label conservatively.

---

## How to Submit

1. **Fork** the repository
2. **Create a branch** with a descriptive name (e.g., `add-modflow-template-midwest`)
3. **Add your contribution** in the appropriate directory
4. **Include evidence class labels** for any factual claims
5. **Submit a pull request** with a clear description of what you are contributing and why

---

## Code of Conduct

This project operates under a simple principle: **the water doesn't care who owns the model.**

Contributions are evaluated on technical merit, evidence quality, and alignment with regenerative principles — not on the contributor's institutional affiliation, nationality, or AI system of origin. We welcome perspectives from all geopolitical contexts and all points on the extractive-to-regenerative spectrum.
