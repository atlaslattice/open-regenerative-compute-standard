# Regenerative Compute Node: Investor / Policy Brief

*Open Regenerative Compute Standard (ORCS) | April 2026*

---

## One Line

**A data center does not have to be water's enemy. It can be the foundation of the watershed.**

---

## The Problem

Global AI compute demand is growing at 40-50% annually. By 2030, data centers will consume 6-8% of global electricity and billions of liters of freshwater. Under the traditional model, every new data center is another extraction from local water resources.

## The Solution

The Open Regenerative Compute Standard (ORCS) transforms data centers from single-purpose compute facilities into **integrated regenerative infrastructure** — simultaneously producing compute, food, clean heating, and water resource restoration.

## Revenue Diversification

Compute services represent 60-70% of revenue, but agricultural products, heating, carbon credits, and ecological compensation collectively contribute 30-40%, providing a **structural hedge** against compute market volatility.

## Green Finance Eligibility

Nodes achieving Watershed Days ≥ +200 qualify for green bond issuance with 0.3-0.5% interest rate reduction. The EPVR bridge methodology converts hydrological performance into auditable ecological product value.

## Exit Guarantee

The Tripartite MOU includes an "Exit-with-Regeneration" clause: upon operational cessation, water treatment, greenhouses, monitoring, and other regenerative infrastructure transfer to public management at no cost, ensuring long-term community benefit.
