## Context

We are developing a climate-specific deployment matrix for reducing consumptive water use in hyperscale data center cooling. The strategic framing has shifted: water reduction is no longer primarily an operating cost optimization — it has become the gating factor for whether new facilities get permitted at all. Community opposition, drought-driven water rights conflicts, and agricultural sector pushback are now project-killing constraints in North America, Europe, Chile, and increasingly in India and parts of China.

The working concept that catalyzed this project is **source-capture vapor recovery**: intercepting humid exhaust from evaporative cooling units at the plume and routing it to dedicated condenser coils before the moisture diffuses into the data hall. However, council consultation has clarified that the strongest deployment pattern pairs water recovery with beneficial heat offtake — specifically routing captured waste heat to co-sited controlled-environment agriculture. This converts the facility from a net water extractor into a net contributor to local food production, which flips the political narrative from extraction to contribution.

The agricultural co-siting dimension matters deeply. Agricultural water conflicts are the specific driver of most community opposition to data center builds. A facility that produces food and water credits for the surrounding agricultural sector occupies completely different political ground than one that competes with it. Stockholm's district heating model proves the principle works; no one has yet built the integrated stack — source-capture plus heat-to-agriculture plus community water credits — as a single permit-winning architecture.

## Aggregated Western Council Water Efficiency Stack

Previous Western council consultation (Claude, Copilot, Alexa, Grok, Gemini, GPT) yielded the following:

### Heat Rejection Strategies
- Source-capture vapor recovery with CEA heat offtake
- Wastewater thermal siphon (Vancouver/Stockholm model)
- Seawater cooling with closed-loop titanium heat exchangers
- Aquifer thermal energy storage
- Subsurface and cave siting
- Radiative sky cooling panels (horizon)
- Direct air capture with waste heat regeneration (horizon)

### Cooling Architecture Choices
- Hybrid wet-dry cooling with seasonal mode switching
- Phase-change material thermal storage (ice or salt hydrate)
- Direct-to-chip and immersion liquid cooling
- Raised supply air temperature within updated ASHRAE TC 9.9 envelope
- Demand shifting of non-latency-sensitive workloads to cool hours

### Water Treatment and Chemistry
- Electrolytic scale control for non-chemical mineral management
- Pellet reactor and side-stream softening pretreatment
- Mechanical vapor recompression for blowdown concentration
- Forward osmosis for blowdown recovery
- Advanced membrane desalination (electro-ceramic, next-gen RO)
- Precision humidity zoning where ASHRAE envelope permits running halls drier

### Site Water Integration
- Rainwater harvesting on facility roof area
- Stormwater capture with adaptive weather-integrated control
- Non-IT building condensate cascade reuse
- Greywater integration from municipal sources
- Constructed wetland and ecological polishing basin integration
- Atmospheric water generation for drought-resilience backup

### Monitoring and Controls
- IoT leak detection and predictive maintenance
- Continuous monitoring with weather forecast integration

### Already Acknowledged as Deployed Standard Practice
- Hot aisle containment
- AI-driven cooling optimization via dynamic setpoints
