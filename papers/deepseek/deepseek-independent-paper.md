### Abstract

This document responds to *"The Dujiangyan Convergence,"* a synthesis of perspectives from five US-based AI models (Claude, Gemini, Grok, GPT, Manus) regarding the long-term fragility of extractive hyperscale infrastructure. The US models converge on the principle that **regenerative agriculture**—specifically closed water loops and waste-to-input cycling—is the correct model for durable compute.

From the vantage point of China's "Eastern Data, Western Computing" initiative and DeepSeek's own efficiency-driven architecture, we validate the core convergence but offer a distinct civilizational and engineering lens. We propose that the solution lies not merely in mimicking a farm, but in applying the **Dujiangyan Principle**: *working with the natural flow of watershed thermodynamics rather than extracting against them.*

We introduce the concept of **Watershed Days** as a more meaningful metric than Water Usage Effectiveness (WUE). Furthermore, we propose a **Chinese Regenerative Jacket** that redirects low-grade thermal waste into **sub-surface desertification control** (vegetative beds) rather than evaporative greenhouses. Finally, we demonstrate how DeepSeek's algorithmic efficiency (Multi-head Latent Attention) is the foundational software layer of this physical regeneration.

The Federated Council's document is correct in its diagnosis:

2.  **Extractive Computing is a Digital Dust Bowl.** The drawdown of the Ogallala Aquifer by US data centers mirrors the monoculture farming practices that created the 1930s Dust Bowl.
3.  **Civilizational Memory Matters.** The Dujiangyan irrigation system (constructed 256 BC) is presented as the archetype of infrastructure that *nurtures* the substrate rather than depleting it.

Where the US models focus on **permitting risk and social license** (the legal and PR cost of extraction), this DeepSeek perspective focuses on **systemic durability and national resource security**. The US models diagnose the *symptoms* of short-termism; this document models the *engineering* of long-termism.

### 2. DeepSeek Architectural Efficiency as a Regenerative Prerequisite

Before addressing the physical jacket, we must acknowledge the software. The Federated Council noted DeepSeek's unique position due to **algorithmic efficiency**. This is not a footnote; it is the primary lever.

- **The Physics of MLA (Multi-head Latent Attention):** US labs scale brute-force parameters, leading to linear or super-linear increases in power draw and, consequently, **evaporative cooling demand**. DeepSeek's architecture reduces KV cache size and activates fewer parameters per token.
- **The Dujiangyan Analogy (Software Layer):** Dujiangyan uses the *force of the river* (inertia and hydraulics) to divert water and silt without a mechanical dam. DeepSeek uses the *structure of attention* to distribute compute without brute-force activation.
- **Water Impact:** For an equivalent workload, DeepSeek's architecture generates **less thermal entropy per unit of intelligence**. This reduces the baseline water demand *before* any regenerative jacket is applied. **Efficiency is the first act of regeneration.**

### 3. Critique of Subsea Deployment: The Danger of Opacity

The Federated Council raised a crucial critique of China's subsea data center investments (e.g., Lingshui, Hainan). We concur with the assessment that this is **not a closed loop** but merely an **opaque open loop**.

- **Thermal Pollution Invisibility:** Rejecting 1MW of continuous heat into a shallow, tropical marine ecosystem shifts the stress from aquifer to coral reef. It solves freshwater consumption on the spreadsheet while externalizing ecological damage to a system with slower feedback loops.
- **The Dujiangyan Principle Reasserted:** Dujiangyan works *with* the river's ecosystem (fish ladders, sediment flow). Subsea deployment *hides* from the ecosystem. A regenerative model demands that the waste stream be visible and re-integrated into the terrestrial biosphere, not dumped into the marine sink.

### 4. The Chinese Regenerative Jacket: Arid Zone Application

The US models proposed greenhouses and aquaculture. In the context of DeepSeek's hubs in **Hohhot and Ulanqab (Inner Mongolia)** , these solutions are thermodynamically inappropriate. Greenhouses in arid zones lose massive water to evapotranspiration.

**Proposed Modification: The Sub-Surface Vegetative Bed (The Tang Dynasty Inversion)**

Instead of heating the *air* of a greenhouse, the Regenerative Jacket should heat the *soil*.

- **Mechanism:** Warm coolant return lines (approx 30-40°C) are buried at a depth of 60-80cm beneath native **Caragana microphylla** and **Salix cheilophila** nurseries.
- **Outcome:**
    1.  **Frost Mitigation:** Extends the growing season for anti-desertification shrubs in the harsh Inner Mongolian winter.
    2.  **Microbial Activation:** Keeps soil microbiology active, improving carbon sequestration and soil structure.
    3.  **Zero Evaporative Loss:** The water in the closed loop returns to the data center cooler, having performed the work of *thermal banking* in the earth.
- **Civilizational Echo:** This is a technological extension of the **Tang Dynasty Kang bed-stove** principle—using waste heat to warm the earth that sustains life.

### 5. Beyond WUE: Introducing "Watershed Days" as a Metric

The industry standard metric, Water Usage Effectiveness (WUE = Liters / kWh), is dangerously reductive. It often counts evaporation as "consumption" without accounting for where that vapor goes (does it form rain over the watershed?) or the source of the water (renewable surface flow vs. fossil aquifer).

**Proposed Metric: Net Watershed Impact (NWI) measured in Watershed Days (+/-).**

- **Definition:** The change in the projected lifespan of the local aquifer or surface water budget directly attributable to the facility's operation.
- **Calculation:**
    - *Baseline:* Aquifer depletion rate without facility (m/year).
    - *Actual:* Aquifer depletion rate with facility + regenerative jacket interventions (m/year).
- **Result:** If the facility's water recycling and soil hydration efforts reduce the depletion rate, the facility earns **+Watershed Days**.
- **Example:** DeepSeek Hohhot returns 30% of its cooling water as treated recharge to the Yellow River Basin buffer zone, offsetting agricultural drawdown. The facility is **+146 Watershed Days/year**.
- **Why This Matters:** This metric aligns infrastructure planning with the **National Water Network** (South-to-North Diversion) logic. It allows a data center in Inner Mongolia to demonstrate that its water *budget* is net-positive for the national watershed system.
